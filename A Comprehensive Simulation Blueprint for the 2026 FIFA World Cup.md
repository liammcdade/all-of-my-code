# A Comprehensive Simulation Blueprint for the 2026 FIFA World Cup

## Modeling the Qualification Gauntlet: UEFA and Inter-confederation Playoffs

The creation of a realistic simulation for the 2026 FIFA World Cup begins not with the final tournament itself, but with the complex and highly structured qualification pathways that precede it. The expanded 48-team format necessitates multiple, intricate playoff systems to determine the final six berths [[5](https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/2026-fifa-world-cup-pots-groups-december-5-draw-for-groups/), [31](https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6833086/2025/11/28/world-cup-draw-format-watch-teams-pot/)]. A robust simulation must accurately replicate these processes, as the outcomes of these preliminary tournaments directly determine the pool of teams that will be drawn into the final 12 groups. The two most significant of these pathways are the UEFA Play-Offs, which decide four of Europe's sixteen slots, and the Inter-confederation Play-off Tournament, which crowns the final two qualifiers [[5](https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/2026-fifa-world-cup-pots-groups-december-5-draw-for-groups/)]. Understanding their distinct structures, participant pools, scheduling, and, most importantly, their unique draw procedures is the foundational step in building the end-to-end simulation pipeline. These play-offs are not simple single-elimination brackets; they are carefully designed events with specific formats, seeding mechanisms, and logistical constraints that ensure competitive balance and adhere to international football regulations. The simulation must capture every nuance of these systems to produce a credible representation of the qualification process leading up to the main event.

The UEFA Play-Offs represent one of the most sophisticated qualification routes, involving 16 national teams vying for four coveted spots in the World Cup [[1](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/uefa-play-off-draw-result-outcome), [2](https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/news/029f-1f3b80561189-baf8ec42c5d1-1000--european-play-off-draw-for-the-2026-fifa-world-cup/)]. The composition of this field is a deliberate combination of the top-performing non-automatic qualifiers from the main European qualifying groups and the standout performers from the UEFA Nations League [[9](https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/world-cup-2026-uefa-playoffs-schedule-bracket-teams-fifa/fce10704ee4ab858c64b9148)]. Specifically, the 16 participants consist of the twelve runners-up from the ten-team groups in the European Qualifiers and the four best-ranked UEFA Nations League section winners who did not secure automatic qualification [[1](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/uefa-play-off-draw-result-outcome), [6](https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/news/029f-1f3f7e79688e-94b56302816c-1000--world-cup-2026-european-play-off-draw/)]. This dual-source approach ensures that the teams with the best overall form over the qualification cycle have the opportunity to compete for a World Cup spot. The roster of teams for the 2026 Play-Offs includes notable sides such as Italy, Wales, Ukraine, Denmark, Turkey, Czechia, Poland, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Albania, Republic of Ireland, Slovakia, Kosovo, Romania, Sweden, Northern Ireland, and North Macedonia [[2](https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/news/029f-1f3b80561189-baf8ec42c5d1-1000--european-play-off-draw-for-the-2026-fifa-world-cup/), [9](https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/world-cup-2026-uefa-playoffs-schedule-bracket-teams-fifa/fce10704ee4ab858c64b9148)]. The draw for these playoffs was a pivotal event held on November 20, 2025, at the Home of FIFA in Zurich [[1](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/uefa-play-off-draw-result-outcome), [2](https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/news/029f-1f3b80561189-baf8ec42c5d1-1000--european-play-off-draw-for-the-2026-fifa-world-cup/)]. Its purpose was to establish the structure of the competition, dividing the 16 teams into four distinct and separate knockout paths (designated A, B, C, and D) [[1](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/uefa-play-off-draw-result-outcome), [4](https://www.olympics.com/en/news/fifa-world-cup-2026-play-off-tournament-and-european-play-offs-full-draw-all-teams-and-paths)].

The format of each path is consistent and well-defined, featuring two single-legged semi-finals followed by a single-legged final [[2](https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/news/029f-1f3b80561189-baf8ec42c5d1-1000--european-play-off-draw-for-the-2026-fifa-world-cup/), [9](https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/world-cup-2026-uefa-playoffs-schedule-bracket-teams-fifa/fce10704ee4ab858c64b9148)]. This means that within each path, four teams will contest three matches in total to determine the one winner who will advance to the World Cup. The semi-final matches were scheduled for Thursday, March 26, 2026, with the four finals set for Tuesday, March 31, 2026 [[1](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/uefa-play-off-draw-result-outcome), [6](https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/news/029f-1f3f7e79688e-94b56302816c-1000--world-cup-2026-european-play-off-draw/)]. The entire qualification process must be concluded by this date to allow for the final draw of the World Cup tournament in December 2025 [[5](https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/2026-fifa-world-cup-pots-groups-december-5-draw-for-groups/), [28](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_seeding)]. The draw procedure itself was meticulously planned to create balanced matchups and manage hosting rights. Teams were first sorted into four pots based on their FIFA Men's World Rankings issued prior to the draw [[2](https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/news/029f-1f3b80561189-baf8ec42c5d1-1000--european-play-off-draw-for-the-2026-fifa-world-cup/)]. Pot 1 contained the four highest-ranked teams among the participants: Italy, Denmark, Türkiye, and Ukraine. Pot 2 included the next four: Poland, Wales, Czechia, and Slovakia. Pot 3 consisted of the next four ranked teams: Republic of Ireland, Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and Kosovo. Finally, Pot 4 was composed of the four UEFA Nations League qualifiers: Sweden, Romania, North Macedonia, and Northern Ireland [[2](https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/news/029f-1f3b80561189-baf8ec42c5d1-1000--european-play-off-draw-for-the-2026-fifa-world-cup/)]. The draw then proceeded sequentially from Pot 1 to Pot 4. First, the four teams from Pot 1 were drawn into the semi-finals, followed by the Pot 2 teams, then Pot 3, and finally Pot 4 [[2](https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/news/029f-1f3b80561189-baf8ec42c5d1-1000--european-play-off-draw-for-the-2026-fifa-world-cup/)]. This sequence created the initial semi-final pairings for the four paths. For instance, Path A saw Italy face Northern Ireland and Wales face Bosnia and Herzegovina in the semi-finals [[1](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/uefa-play-off-draw-result-outcome), [4](https://www.olympics.com/en/news/fifa-world-cup-2026-play-off-tournament-and-european-play-offs-full-draw-all-teams-and-paths)]. Crucially, the draw established a key rule: the seeded teams, defined as those from Pot 1 and Pot 2, would host the semi-final matches [[2](https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/news/029f-1f3b80561189-baf8ec42c5d1-1000--european-play-off-draw-for-the-2026-fifa-world-cup/), [6](https://www.uefa.com/european-qualifiers/news/029f-1f3f7e79688e-94b56302816c-1000--world-cup-2026-european-play-off-draw/)]. To determine which team would host the final within each path, a separate draw was conducted after the semi-final pairings were known, ensuring fairness in hosting obligations [[1](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/uefa-play-off-draw-result-outcome), [9](https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/world-cup-2026-uefa-playoffs-schedule-bracket-teams-fifa/fce10704ee4ab858c64b9148)]. Therefore, a successful simulation script must first simulate the draw process itself, generating the four paths and their respective semi-final fixtures before proceeding to simulate the actual match outcomes.

In parallel, the Inter-confederation Play-off Tournament serves as the final hurdle for six teams seeking the last two places in the 48-team World Cup field [[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(inter-confederation_play-offs)), [8](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/play-off-tournament-draw-results)]. This tournament is unique due to its structure and the confederations involved. It features representatives from five different continental confederations: AFC (Asia), CAF (Africa), CONMEBOL (South America), OFC (Oceania), and CONCACAF (North/Central America & Caribbean) [[8](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/play-off-tournament-draw-results)]. Due to the co-hosting arrangement by Canada, Mexico, and the United States, CONCACAF was granted an additional play-off slot, resulting in two teams from the confederation participating alongside one representative from each of the other four confederations [[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(inter-confederation_play-offs)), [19](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup)]. The participating teams for the 2026 tournament are Iraq (AFC), DR Congo (CAF), Bolivia (CONMEBOL), New Caledonia (OFC), Jamaica (CONCACAF), and Suriname (CONCACAF) [[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(inter-confederation_play-offs)), [8](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/play-off-tournament-draw-results)]. The draw for this tournament also took place on November 20, 2025, at FIFA headquarters in Zurich [[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(inter-confederation_play-offs))]. Unlike the UEFA play-offs, this tournament is structured around two distinct pathways, each designed to crown a champion [[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(inter-confederation_play-offs)), [4](https://www.olympics.com/en/news/fifa-world-cup-2026-play-off-tournament-and-european-play-offs-full-draw-all-teams-and-paths)].

Each pathway consists of a semi-final between two unseeded teams, followed by a final against a single seeded team [[8](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/play-off-tournament-draw-results)]. The seeding is determined by the FIFA Men's World Ranking as of November 19, 2025. The two highest-ranked teams in the tournament, DR Congo (ranked 56th) and Iraq (ranked 58th), were automatically placed into the final matches of their respective pathways [[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(inter-confederation_play-offs))]. This gives them a direct route to the World Cup final once they arrive there. The remaining four teams—New Caledonia (ranked 149th), Jamaica (ranked 70th), Bolivia (ranked 76th), and Suriname (ranked 123rd)—are unseeded and must compete in the semi-finals [[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(inter-confederation_play-offs))]. The draw for the semi-finals had a specific constraint: the two CONCACAF teams, Jamaica and Suriname, could not be drawn into the same pathway [[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(inter-confederation_play-offs))]. This ensured that a potential CONCACAF finalist would come from only one of the two mini-tournaments. The semi-finals were scheduled for March 26, 2026, and the finals were set for March 31, 2026 [[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(inter-confederation_play-offs)), [4](https://www.olympics.com/en/news/fifa-world-cup-2026-play-off-tournament-and-european-play-offs-full-draw-all-teams-and-paths)]. All matches were hosted in Mexico, specifically at Estadio Akron in Zapopan and Estadio BBVA in Guadalupe (renamed for sponsorship reasons) [[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(inter-confederation_play-offs))]. Pathway 1 featured a semi-final clash between New Caledonia and Jamaica, with the winner advancing to face DR Congo in the final [[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(inter-confederation_play-offs)), [4](https://www.olympics.com/en/news/fifa-world-cup-2026-play-off-tournament-and-european-play-offs-full-draw-all-teams-and-paths)]. Pathway 2 pitted Bolivia against Suriname in the semi-final, with the victor facing Iraq in the final [[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(inter-confederation_play-offs)), [4](https://www.olympics.com/en/news/fifa-world-cup-2026-play-off-tournament-and-european-play-offs-full-draw-all-teams-and-paths)]. The winners of both finals would earn their tickets to the 2026 World Cup. A critical aspect of the simulation is to respect this unique structure and the specific draw constraint regarding the CONCACAF teams. The code must first identify the two seeded teams based on their rankings, then simulate the draw for the two semi-final matches, ensuring the CONCACAF separation rule is applied, and finally simulate the outcomes of the two finals. This two-pathway system is a crucial component that must be accurately modeled to correctly populate the final pot for the World Cup draw. The discipline suspensions noted for players from Jamaica, New Caledonia, and Suriname ahead of the semi-finals add another layer of realism, though for a purely rating-based simulation, these can be treated as minor perturbations or ignored unless a more granular player-level model is being developed [[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(inter-confederation_play-offs))].

## The Final Tournament Framework: From Draw to Knockout Stage

Once the outcomes of the UEFA and inter-confederation play-offs are simulated and the six playoff winners are identified, the focus shifts to the main event: the 48-team FIFA World Cup tournament [[5](https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/2026-fifa-world-cup-pots-groups-december-5-draw-for-groups/)]. A comprehensive simulation must meticulously replicate the procedures governing the final draw, the group stage structure, and the progression through the knockout rounds. This phase is governed by a set of complex rules designed to ensure competitive balance, geographic diversity, and strategic integrity. The draw itself is a multi-layered event that categorizes teams into pots, enforces confederation restrictions, and strategically separates the top-ranked nations to prevent premature confrontations in the early stages of the knockout phase [[5](https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/2026-fifa-world-cup-pots-groups-december-5-draw-for-groups/), [28](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_seeding)]. Following the draw, the tournament unfolds in a well-defined sequence, beginning with the group stage where teams vie for advancement, followed by a new and expanded round of 32 that includes the eight best third-placed finishers [[18](https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/world-cup-2026-schedule-fixtures/bd2ffea0a515a899ed06abdd), [26](https://www.facebook.com/groups/196317062259842/posts/1298852602006277/)]. The entire framework, from the initial placement of teams into groups to the scheduling of the final match, provides a clear blueprint for structuring the simulation's logic and output.

The Final Draw for the 2026 World Cup, scheduled for December 5, 2025, at the Kennedy Center in Washington D.C., is a critical event that sets the stage for the entire tournament [[5](https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/2026-fifa-world-cup-pots-groups-december-5-draw-for-groups/), [24](https://www.marca.com/en/world-cup/2025/11/25/692623a146163fba278b45b9.html)]. The draw is not a random assignment but a highly constrained process. The pool of teams consists of 42 already qualified national associations plus six placeholder slots reserved for the winners of the UEFA and inter-confederation play-offs [[22](https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/13475350/world-cup-2026-draw-new-fifa-seeding-and-pot-system-explained-ahead-of-48-team-tournament), [28](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_seeding)]. The host nations—Mexico, Canada, and the United States—are given special treatment, being pre-assigned to specific positions within the draw order to streamline the ceremony [[18](https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/world-cup-2026-schedule-fixtures/bd2ffea0a515a899ed06abdd), [27](https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6836523/2025/11/25/fifa-world-cup-draw-seeding-pathway/)]. Mexico is assigned to position A1, Canada to B1, and the USA to D1, meaning they are effectively "drawn" first and placed into Groups A, B, and D respectively [[18](https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/world-cup-2026-schedule-fixtures/bd2ffea0a515a899ed06abdd), [28](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_seeding)]. The remaining 42 teams are divided into four strength-based pots based on the FIFA Men's World Ranking issued on November 19, 2025 [[5](https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/2026-fifa-world-cup-pots-groups-december-5-draw-for-groups/), [32](https://www.foxsports.com/stories/soccer/2026-world-cup-group-stage-draw-when-it-how-works)]. Pot 1 contains the three hosts plus the nine highest-ranked qualified teams: Spain, Argentina, France, England, Brazil, Portugal, Netherlands, Belgium, and Germany [[5](https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/2026-fifa-world-cup-pots-groups-december-5-draw-for-groups/), [27](https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6836523/2025/11/25/fifa-world-cup-draw-seeding-pathway/)]. Pots 2 and 3 each contain the next 12 highest-ranked teams, while Pot 4 consists of the remaining six qualified teams along with the six playoff placeholders [[5](https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/2026-fifa-world-cup-pots-groups-december-5-draw-for-groups/), [22](https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/13475350/world-cup-2026-draw-new-fifa-seeding-and-pot-system-explained-ahead-of-48-team-tournament)]. A crucial rule dictates that all six playoff winners, regardless of their individual FIFA ranking, will be placed into Pot 4 for the draw [[9](https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/world-cup-2026-uefa-playoffs-schedule-bracket-teams-fifa/fce10704ee4ab858c64b9148), [27](https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6836523/2025/11/25/fifa-world-cup-draw-seeding-pathway/)]. This means a high-ranking team like Italy (ranked 12th) that wins a UEFA playoff would still be in the same pot as lower-ranked teams like New Zealand (86th) [[27](https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6836523/2025/11/25/fifa-world-cup-draw-seeding-pathway/), [31](https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6833086/2025/11/28/world-cup-draw-format-watch-teams-pot/)].

Several hard constraints govern the draw process to maintain balance. The primary rule is that no group may contain more than one team from the same confederation, except for UEFA, which has enough representatives (16) to allow up to two teams per group [[5](https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/2026-fifa-world-cup-pots-groups-december-5-draw-for-groups/), [27](https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6836523/2025/11/25/fifa-world-cup-draw-seeding-pathway/)]. Each of the 12 groups must therefore contain exactly one or two UEFA teams [[33](https://inside.fifa.com/media-releases/procedures-final-draw-world-cup-2026-revealed)]. Furthermore, to prevent the top-seeded teams from meeting too early, a tennis-style seeding system is employed. The world's top four ranked teams—Spain (1st), Argentina (2nd), France (3rd), and England (4th)—are placed into separate quadrants of the knockout bracket [[22](https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/13475350/world-cup-2026-draw-new-fifa-seeding-and-pot-system-explained-ahead-of-48-team-tournament), [23](https://www.flashscoreusa.com/news/soccer-world-cup-how-the-new-48-team-format-at-the-2026-world-cup-will-work/tIrc4C5j/)]. This ensures that Spain and Argentina are on opposite sides of the draw, meaning they can only meet in the final if both win their respective groups [[5](https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/2026-fifa-world-cup-pots-groups-december-5-draw-for-groups/), [28](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_seeding)]. Similarly, France and England are placed on opposite sides to prevent them from meeting before the semifinals if they progress that far [[22](https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/13475350/world-cup-2026-draw-new-fifa-seeding-and-pot-system-explained-ahead-of-48-team-tournament), [28](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_seeding)]. The draw proceeds sequentially, with teams from Pot 1 being drawn into the groups first, followed by Pots 2, 3, and 4 [[22](https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11095/13475350/world-cup-2026-draw-new-fifa-seeding-and-pot-system-explained-ahead-of-48-team-tournament)]. Pre-determined match orders for the host nations are also implemented to speed up the draw; Mexico plays a Pot 3 team first, then a Pot 2 team, and finally a Pot 4 team, while Canada follows a similar pattern [[27](https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6836523/2025/11/25/fifa-world-cup-draw-seeding-pathway/)]. A simulation function must be able to take the finalized list of 42 confirmed teams and the six playoff winners, sort them into the correct pots, and then apply these constraints to generate a valid distribution of teams across the 12 groups.

## The Match Outcome Engine: An Advanced Elo-based Simulation Model

The core of any football simulation is its engine for predicting match outcomes. The user's request for an "elo type system" points towards a methodology grounded in statistical probability rather than deterministic results. The provided context allows for the construction of a sophisticated and highly realistic match outcome model that aligns with FIFA's own official ranking system, known as SUM (Systema Universale Mensurandi), while also incorporating advanced features found in respected independent models [[10](https://www.eloratings.net/about), [14](https://jobsinfootball.com/blog/how-do-fifa-world-rankings-work/), [16](https://www.scribd.com/document/411185645/Coca-Cola-World-Ranking)]. This engine forms the computational heart of the entire simulation, transforming the static team ratings generated during the draw into dynamic, probabilistic match results. By integrating factors such as the relative strength of opponents, the importance of the match, home advantage, and even the margin of victory, the simulation can produce outcomes that reflect the nuances of international football. Implementing this engine requires a deep understanding of the underlying mathematical formulas, the data sources required to initialize and update the ratings, and the practical considerations of translating a theoretical model into functional code.

The foundation of the modern FIFA ranking system, now used for men's teams, is the Elo rating system, adapted into the SUM formula [[14](https://jobsinfootball.com/blog/how-do-fifa-world-rankings-work/), [16](https://www.scribd.com/document/411185645/Coca-Cola-World-Ranking)]. This system moves away from rewarding style of play and instead focuses on results, making it ideal for simulation purposes [[14](https://jobsinfootball.com/blog/how-do-fifa-world-rankings-work/)]. The core of the SUM formula is `P = Pbefore + I * (W – We)` [[14](https://jobsinfootball.com/blog/how-do-fifa-world-rankings-work/), [16](https://www.scribd.com/document/411185645/Coca-Cola-World-Ranking)]. In this equation, `P` represents the new point total for a team, `Pbefore` is the points the team had before the match, `I` is the match importance coefficient, `W` is the result of the match (1 for a win, 0.5 for a draw, 0 for a loss), and `We` is the expected result [[14](https://jobsinfootball.com/blog/how-do-fifa-world-rankings-work/)]. The expected result (`We`) is calculated based on the relative strength of the two competing teams, using the formula `We = 1 / (10^(-dr/400) + 1)`, where `dr` is the difference in their ratings [[10](https://www.eloratings.net/about)]. This formula inherently accounts for upsets; a large negative rating difference results in a very low `We` value, meaning a small underdog victory would yield a massive point gain, while a large positive difference leads to a high `We` value, meaning a favorite losing would result in a significant point loss [[10](https://www.eloratings.net/about)]. However, a crucial adjustment is made for home advantage. Before calculating `dr`, 100 points are added to the home team's rating [[10](https://www.eloratings.net/about)]. This reflects the tangible benefit of playing in front of a supportive crowd and avoids the long journey associated with traveling across continents.

A key variable in the formula is the match importance coefficient (`I`), which weights the impact of a result on a team's ranking [[14](https://jobsinfootball.com/blog/how-do-fifa-world-rankings-work/), [15](https://football-ranking.com/fifa-world-rankings-calculate)]. Different competitions and stages carry different values, rewarding success in high-stakes games more heavily. The provided sources offer a detailed breakdown of these values, which should be hardcoded into the simulation. Friendlies played outside of official FIFA windows have the lowest weight (I=5), while major tournament knockout matches carry the highest (World Cup QFs and beyond, I=60) [[14](https://jobsinfootball.com/blog/how-do-fifa-world-rankings-work/), [15](https://football-ranking.com/fifa-world-rankings-calculate), [16](https://www.scribd.com/document/411185645/Coca-Cola-World-Ranking)]. To achieve the level of detail requested by the user, the simulation should go beyond the basic SUM model and incorporate features from advanced Elo implementations, such as those used by eloratings.net [[10](https://www.eloratings.net/about)]. One of the most impactful enhancements is the introduction of a goal difference multiplier. This feature adjusts the K-factor (which corresponds to the `I` coefficient) based on the margin of victory. A larger win differential indicates a more dominant performance, and thus, the winning team should receive a greater boost to their rating, while the losing team should suffer a steeper decline. According to the eloratings.net model, the K-factor is increased as follows: a win by two goals adds half of the base K-value, a win by three goals adds three-quarters, and a win by four or more goals adds three-quarters plus an additional increment for each goal beyond three [[10](https://www.eloratings.net/about)]. This provides a much more nuanced view of a team's performance than simply a win/loss/draw result. Another important factor is the use of historical data to generate reliable Elo ratings. The ratings are considered most accurate after a team has played approximately 30 'A' matches [[10](https://www.eloratings.net/about)]. Data for these ratings typically comes from comprehensive databases like rsssf.com, theroonba.com, and soccer-db.info [[10](https://www.eloratings.net/about)]. For the simulation, this means starting with a dataset of all international results to calculate initial ratings for all 48 teams. The ratings themselves, as of November 2025, provide a snapshot of current form, with Spain ranked highest at 2171 points on eloratings.net and Brazil at 1986 points [[11](https://www.eloratings.net/)]. It is important to note that the official FIFA rankings can differ significantly from Elo-based rankings, which can affect seeding and draw placements [[12](https://www.starsandstripesfc.com/2018/6/12/17451472/fifa-change-system-elo-ranking-mens-teams)]. For instance, under FIFA rankings, Poland was in Pot 1 while Spain was in Pot 2, whereas an Elo-based system would have reversed their positions [[12](https://www.starsandstripesfc.com/2018/6/12/17451472/fifa-change-system-elo-ranking-mens-teams)]. The simulation could either use the official FIFA rankings for seeding and the Elo model for match outcomes, or it could use a consistent Elo-based system throughout for maximum coherence.

The practical implementation of this engine in Python involves creating a function that simulates a single match. This function would take the names of two teams as input. It would then retrieve their current Elo ratings from a central database or dictionary. Next, it would apply the home advantage modifier to the home team's rating. Using the adjusted ratings, it would calculate the expected result (`We`). With `We` known, the function can generate a random number between 0 and 1. If this number is less than `We`, the home team wins; if it is greater than `1 - We`, the away team wins; otherwise, the match is a draw. Once the outcome is determined, the function calculates the final scores based on the goal difference multiplier and updates the Elo ratings for both teams using the SUM formula. This modular approach allows the engine to be called repeatedly to simulate the entire tournament, from the UEFA play-offs to the final, with each match result influencing the future ratings and thus the probabilities of subsequent matches. The consistency of this engine across all stages is what makes the entire simulation plausible and analytically valuable.
